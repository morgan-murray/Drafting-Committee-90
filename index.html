Dear Marco, There are a couple of general points to bear in mind with our replies to your comments. The first is that the associated fraction in each bin is poorly known; we used three parameterisations that produced a 20-30% variance between them and picked Brasse as the middle one. So, a lot of your concerns about the associated fractions in each bin and with different geometries are difficult to answer from that point of view, because the genuine answer is that we don't really know enough to construct an accurate MC model. The second is about beam slopes. Elke and Zhenyu produced an internal report that detailed the affect that beam slopes may have on DVCS asymmetries. The conclusion from their work is more or less that the beam slopes have a very, very small effect. To see an effect in the TTSA amplitudes, one needs to have a beam slope in the MC about 10 times that expected in the experiment. So, although we have not done dedicated work there (neither have DC69, 80, 81, 91 or 92), we're not concerned by that.
